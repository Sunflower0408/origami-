Interesting Facts About the Camel

  • Camels have 3 sets of eyelids and 2 sets of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • The humps of camels is used to store fat-not water
  • There are three types of camels in the world: dromedary camels (or Arabian camels), Bactrian camels (or Asian camels), and wild Bactrian camels (Camelus ferus).

Interesting Facts About the Chameleon

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder
  • Changing skin color is an important part of communication amoung chameleons
  • Most Chameleons have a prehensile trail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Intersting Facts About the Pigeon

  • Pigeons were the first domestic bird
  • They can find their way back to their nest from 1,300 miles away
  • Pigeons can be many colors or one solid color

Interesting Facts About the Teddy Bear

  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff
  • The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’
  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.

Interesting Facts About the Panda

  • They have great camouflage for their environment
  • Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo
  • Pandas sometimes does handstands while it uses the bathroom

Interesting Facts About the Cicada

  • Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans
  • Their lengthy life cycles may help them evade predators
  • Cicadas inundate forests as a survival mechanism
